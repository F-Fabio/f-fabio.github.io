A little about me...

I am an econometrics enthusiast.

Currently, I am a Data Scientist at Qi4M.

Prior to joining the Qi4M company, I was a PhD trainee at the European Central Bank. Additionally, I was a visiting researcher at Penn State University.

Previously, I had the opportunity to teach econometrics and time series analysis to graduate students at the University of Rome Tor Vergata.

However in my former life, prior to my career in Academia, I was a professional kayaker for the Marina Militare Italiana. I devoted several years to my kayak and paddles racing across Europe.

I received my Bachelors in Business Administration and my Masters degree in Economics, both were obtained from the University of Cassino and Southern Lazio. I went on to further my studies at the University of Rome Tor Vergata where I earned a second M.S. as well as my Ph.D. in Economics concentrated on the fields of Econometrics and Statistics. Likewise, I participated in an auxillary graduate program in Econometrics at the Einaudi Institute of Economics and Finance.

Within the wide world of econometrics my focus is on time series analysis of linear/nonlinear dynamic systems. From there I dive deeper into estimating macroeconomic and financial models including semi-structural, DSGE and stochastic volatility models. My most recent interest is using artificial intelligence to anticipate future dynamics of financial markets.
